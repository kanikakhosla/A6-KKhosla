Mental Health

Art therapy encourages creativity and self-expression; sensations that are generally suppressed and not experiences in the conventional workplace. It can, however, be used to counter those negative emotions that are experienced within the workplace: Art therapy is a portable form of therapy, and one that can be practiced alone or collectively.

Relationships

The usefulness of the art therapy modality in making visible the covert messages and hidden edicts from the family of origin will be illustrated. Through use of art materials couples are given an opportunity to solve problems in a manner which is unique and unfamiliar. The challenge of art tasks leads the couple to problem-solving and a creative approach in mastering a change in their relationship.

Abuse & Trauma

Using art to express emotion accesses both visually stored memory and body memory, as not only does it enable people to create images, but the use of art materials such as clay and paint can reconnect them to physical sensation. They may be cut off from their bodily senses, particularly in cases of physical and sexual abuse. Express yourself

You will gain an understanding of how much of a role emotion plays in artmaking as they create paintings based on feelings.

MATERIALS:

  • Acrylic Paint
  • Paint Brush
  • Something to paint on: canvas, hand, paper etc.
  • Background Music

WHAT TO DO:
Take a deep breath - and paint how you feel at that moment. It could be anything - a single dot on a paper or a Andy Warhol Painting. Once you feel like you are in the groove, put on some jazz music and paint to the emotion of the music.Play around with different lines - straight, jagged, squiggly, zig-zag, etc.

Help each other

You'll get a sense of unity when you collaborate with others to do something creative

MATERIALS:

  • Cardboard boxes or paper/newspaper
  • Hot glue gun or tape
  • Box cutter or scissors
  • A group of 3+ people

WHAT TO DO:
Use pieces of paper or boxes to create the craziest looking airplane your group can think of.

Color Color Color

The best way to relax - colouring helps one just focus in on one element and forget about the rest of the world.

MATERIALS:

  • Color pencils or pens or crayons
  • Print out of a coloring book sheet
  • A comfortable seat
  • Background Music

WHAT TO DO:
Start coloring in one corner and then slowly move along the picture. If you feel frustrated feel free to jump around the picture. If you don't know where to start, choose a colour scheme: cool colours (blue, green, purple) or warm colors (red, orange, yellow).

Get creative with words

Words help us formulate our thoughts. Here we are going to practice the opposite: word vomit.

MATERIALS:

  • Something to write with
  • Something to write on
  • Relatively quiet area

WHAT TO DO:
Close your eyes and when you open your eyes write down the first thought that comes your mind and continue writing. Don't check for grammar or spelling. If a thought process ends start with another one. Your writing can be as disjointed or put together as you like - don't stress about it, just continue writing till all those thoughts in your mind aren't on paper. These can be blurbs, lists, letters, notes, alphabets - anything that is in your mind.

Scribble Away

There is so much you can do with a simple scribble. Get creative!

MATERIALS:

  • Pen or Pencil
  • Paper
  • A hard surface - desk, book etc. - to rest the paper on

WHAT TO DO:
Start scribbling for about 2 minutes - light, hard, soft - all different types of scribbles. After you are warmed up draw an eye or the sunset with scribbles by never lifting up your pen.
